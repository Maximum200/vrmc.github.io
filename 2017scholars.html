Q2. What made you apply for this scholarship?

In JC I found out that I enjoyed doing research. I also was frankly speaking unsure of whether I want to be an engineer who works mostly on the ground or behind the scenes in a laboratory. As a result, I wanted to gain exposure to research while pursuing an engineering degree (NTU Chemical and Biomolecular Engineering). Furthermore, knowing that a research job will require a PhD, this scholars programme allows me to attain a PhD earlier as I can bypass getting a Master's Degree.

Teo Hong Ray

teohongray@gmail.com

Q1: What is one fond memory you have of VRMC?

I remember the countless test flights we conducted for our remote controlled plane. Despite repeated and often embarrassing crashes, our enthusiasm did not waver and we quickly moved to repair and fine tune the plane for its next flight. With such a passionate team, I felt as if we were the Wright Brothers who were determined to make our plane work.



Q2. What made you apply for this scholarship?

I've always had an interest in weapons and the military, being the kind of guy who modifies NERF guns and coming from an NCC background. Furthermore, DSTA is a prestigious organisation contributing to the important task of national security.



Q3. Did your experiences in VRMC help you in attaining this scholarship?

Definitely! VRMC had exposed me to so many different areas that helped to build up my portfolio, from engineering physics to programming to 3d printing. Without a doubt, VRMC is the goto place if you want to further your interests in tech.



Q4. What is one thing you're passionate about?

I like creating things with arduino with the endless possibilities it presents. Looking into the future, I believe that such embedded systems would play a big role in the form of IoT devices.



Q5. What is one piece of advice you wish you knew at 18 years old?

Treasure the friends around you in school, because such people can be hard to come by in the outside world.
